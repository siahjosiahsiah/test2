Speaking Telegraph

Speaking telegraphs were early communication devices that allowed users to transmit spoken words over long distances using telegraph lines. These inventions marked a significant milestone in the development of telecommunications technology, with notable examples like the "harmonium" and "phonautograph" in the 19th century. Users could speak into a microphone-like device, and their voice vibrations were converted into electrical signals and transmitted along telegraph wires to a receiving station. Although speaking telegraphs were precursors to modern telephony, they played a pivotal role in the evolution of voice communication technology.
